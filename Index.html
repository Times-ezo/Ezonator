THE MATHEMATICS OF ENCRYPTION

Modern encryption relies on number theory, specifically the computational difficulty of factoring large prime numbers. RSA encryption, developed in 1977, uses the product of two large primes as a public key. Breaking this would require solving the integer factorization problem — currently infeasible with classical computers but potentially vulnerable to quantum algorithms like Shor's algorithm.

ZERO TRUST ARCHITECTURE EXPLAINED

The Zero Trust security model operates on the principle of "never trust, always verify" — no user or device is automatically trusted regardless of network location. Implemented through micro-segmentation, multi-factor authentication, and continuous monitoring, Zero Trust is becoming the gold standard for enterprise security. Understanding this framework is essential for any aspiring cybersecurity professional.
